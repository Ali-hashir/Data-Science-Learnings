Clustering¶

This notebook performs a clustering analysis on McDonald’s menu data. We begin by cleaning the dataset and normalizing the nutritional features using MinMax scaling. Next, we determine the optimal number of clusters using both the Elbow and Silhouette methods, concluding that three clusters best capture the data's structure. We then apply the K-Means algorithm, visualize the resulting clusters along with their centroids, and extract insights by analyzing the average nutritional metrics within each cluster. Finally, we name the clusters based on their key characteristics (e.g., high caloric items, low fat items, and meat-based items).
